Support to get a job or stay in work

Finding a job

Your local Jobcentre can:

  • help you find a job or gain new skills
  • tell you about employers who support disabilities and health conditions in your area
  • explain available programmes and grants to help you back into work
  • SCOPE - get help finding a job if you’re disabled or have a health condition
  • Find work if you’re disabled

Programmes and grants

You might be eligible for the Work and Health programme which helps you:

  • match your skills to work that’s available
  • put you in touch with employers
  • find long-term employment
  • get training to help you find work
  • manage health problems to reduce their impact on work

You might also be able to apply for the Access to Work scheme if you’re in paid work, or about to start or return to paid work in the next 12 weeks.

Through Access to Work, you can apply for:

  • a grant to help pay for practical support in a job
  • money to pay for communication support at job interviews
  • support with managing mental health at work

Reasonable adjustments

You can ask your employer to look at changes you may need at work, known as reasonable adjustments. Your employer has a duty to support you.

You can use the Health Adjustment Passport form when talking with your employer.

It helps you identify help or changes you need. For example, you may need:

  • flexible or part-time working
  • to speak to an occupational health adviser in your organisation
  • time off for health appointments
